01

About

Researcher at heart, I work at the intersection of computer vision and language. As an AI researcher and systems architect at Sorenson Communications, I build sign language translation technology, with specialties in video understanding, visual action recognition, vision-language modeling, and natural language processing.

The systems-architect half of the role means I own the unglamorous machinery that makes research usable: data collection, curation, and clean-up; an optimized training infrastructure; and a low-cost, low-latency inference path that runs vision-language models in real time. Earlier in my career I worked on video, audio, and speech codecs across a range of signal-processing middleware and frameworks. I hold a master's in Computer Science from Arizona State University, focused on computer vision and image processing.

02

Selected work

Real-time ASL recognition, shipped to AI PCs

~93% accuracy · 3 US patents

Helped deliver one of the most accurate sign language recognition systems (~93%), shipped on Intel AI PCs for real-time communication between deaf and hearing users. The work produced three US patents along the way.

Training & inference infrastructure

2 wks → 2 days · 10 → 30 FPS

Built the end-to-end training and inference pipeline for ASL, along with the computer-vision pre-processing behind it. Cut training time from two weeks to two days, and inference from 10 FPS at 6-second latency to ~30 FPS under 2 seconds — fast enough for live conversation. Cross-compatible across Nvidia GPUs, x86 cloud CPUs, and Intel AI PCs on Linux and Windows.

Automated testing at scale

~20K-sample test sets

Created automated testing infrastructure that runs ~20,000-sample test sets against both offline and real-world data, and benchmarked the sets that keep model quality honest.

Sign language data pipeline

2M+ samples

Own the data pipeline end to end — collection, clean-up, annotation checks, video-quality checks, outlier detection, and dataset preparation. Worked across two million samples to balance datasets for training video-to-text, BERT-like models, with monthly quality reporting.

Intel RealSense gesture control

Finalist · Intel RealSense Challenge

Finalist in the Intel RealSense Challenge for a gesture-recognition app that controls a PC hands-free — click, scroll, and basic operations.
